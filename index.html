Accounting Pathways Guide

A student-centered guide designed to help MSOE students better understand accounting careers, CPA pathways, industry opportunities, and the future of the profession.

Project Purpose

The Accounting Pathways Guide is being developed to help students see accounting as a broad, flexible, and technology-connected career path. The guide will provide students with clearer information about accounting roles, CPA requirements, career options, and how accounting connects to data, analytics, business strategy, and emerging technologies.

Problem Statement

Many students have a narrow view of accounting and may not fully understand the variety of career paths available within the profession. Students may also have concerns about how artificial intelligence and automation could affect accounting careers. This project aims to address those concerns by giving students practical, accessible, and current information about the profession.

Target Audience

  • Current accounting students
  • Business students considering accounting
  • Students interested in CPA licensure
  • Advisors and faculty supporting career conversations

Project Goals

Increase Awareness

Help students understand the range of accounting careers beyond traditional stereotypes.

Clarify CPA Pathways

Provide accessible information about CPA requirements, exam planning, and career value.

Connect Accounting to the Future

Address how analytics, technology, and AI are changing accounting work.

Planned Deliverables

  • Published Accounting Pathways Guide
  • In-class presentations for accounting and business students
  • Industry speaker or professional perspective session
  • Optional one-on-one student conversations
  • Student feedback survey before and/or after implementation

Timeline

Summer 2026

Define scope, gather resources, and draft guide structure.

Fall 2026

Develop guide content and identify opportunities for classroom integration.

Spring 2027

Finalize guide, deliver presentations, collect feedback, and evaluate impact.

Measures of Success

  • Guide is completed and shared with students
  • At least three student-facing presentations are delivered
  • At least one industry speaker or professional perspective is included
  • Student feedback shows improved understanding of accounting career options

Project Status

This project is currently in the planning and development stage. The next steps include finalizing the project charter, outlining guide content, identifying key career pathways to feature, and determining how the guide will be shared with students.
